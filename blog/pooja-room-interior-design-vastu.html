Pooja Room Interior Design Vastu

June 10, 202610 min readMoreBuildtech Expert TeamMumbai

In India, a pooja room is an essential part of every home, and pooja room interior design vastu plays a significant role in creating a peaceful and serene atmosphere. When designing a pooja room, it's crucial to consider the principles of Vastu Shastra to ensure a harmonious and balanced space. In Mumbai, where space is limited, it's even more important to optimize the design of your pooja room.

Pooja Room Location and Layout

The location and layout of your pooja room are critical in determining the overall energy of the space. According to Vastu, the pooja room should be located in the north-east direction of the house, which is considered the most sacred direction.

Benefits of North-East Direction

Placing your pooja room in the north-east direction is believed to bring positive energy and prosperity to the family. It's also said to promote spiritual growth and inner peace.

Pooja Room Interior Design Ideas

When it comes to designing the interior of your pooja room, there are several factors to consider. From the color scheme to the lighting, every element should be carefully chosen to create a peaceful and calming atmosphere.

Color Scheme

A soothing color scheme is essential for a pooja room. Shades of white, cream, and light blue are popular choices, as they promote a sense of calmness and serenity.

Vastu-Friendly Pooja Room Decor

The decor of your pooja room should also be chosen with care. Avoid using any items that are considered inauspicious, such as broken or damaged idols, and instead opt for beautiful and meaningful pieces that promote positivity and harmony.

Idol Placement

The placement of idols in your pooja room is also important. According to Vastu, idols should be placed on a pedestal or shelf, and should face the east or west direction.

Pooja Room Lighting

Lighting plays a significant role in creating a peaceful ambiance in your pooja room. Soft, warm lighting is ideal, and can be achieved using table lamps, floor lamps, or string lights.

Pooja Room Storage and Organization

Proper storage and organization are essential for maintaining a clutter-free and peaceful pooja room. Consider using storage units, such as cabinets or shelves, to keep your pooja items and accessories organized.

Cost of Pooja Room Interior Design

The cost of designing a pooja room can vary depending on the size of the room, the materials used, and the complexity of the design. On average, the cost of a pooja room interior design in Mumbai can range from ₹50,000 to ₹200,000 or more.

In Maharashtra, where the cost of living is relatively high, it's essential to work with an experienced interior designer who can help you create a beautiful and functional pooja room within your budget.
